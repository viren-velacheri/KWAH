About

 

A dog makes your house a home ! Koko wants a home is a simple website that makes adopting a dog from the Austin Animal Center, easier and faster. We got this idea when we were adopting our dog Koko. It was difficult to search for dogs on the Austin Animal Center website. It had very primitive search capabilities. It was hard to keep track of which dogs we had already seen, which were the new dogs ,without browsing through the entire list.

Other websites listed so many dogs, it was overwhelming. Also, many were from rescue groups which were difficult to get in touch with to actually see the dogs. At one point we were so tired we were thinking about buying a dog from a breeder. It took us about 3 months to find our puppy. We got him by sheer luck as our neighbor who volunteers at the shelter spotted Koko and felt he might be the perfect dog for us.

After seeing our parents struggle and getting frustrated during the adoption process my brother created Koko wants a Home. Some of Koko wants a Home’s features are: you sign in with your Google or facebook account so you’re able to save your searches and you can also create a list of favorites. This is a major time saver because most shelter’s websites don’t let you save your searches.

Here is an example: Let's say I am looking for a poodle on Austin Animal Center’s website, I find a couple of cuties that I add to my favorites list. I also save off my search. I head over to the shelter, to look at each dog. Like a shopping list you can cross off dogs that aren't a good fit. If you didn't find the dog you were looking for, no worries. Since you have saved your "poodle" search, when a new dog matching your search criteria arrives, you will automatically be notified by email.

We hope this website is a small step in making the adoption process just a little bit more streamlined and efficient, resulting in many more Kokos finding a home!

FAQ

What are some tips for searching ?

Type what you want into the search text window. There aren't a million radio buttons, drop-downs. You can type in breed, size (small, medium, large), age, or the specific ID of the dog. You can combine multiple criteria together. Here are a few examples:

  • Search for all dogs. Leave search field blank
  • Search for puppy/pup (less than 1 year old), type: puppy
  • terrier pup, type: terrier pup
  • Small dog less than 2 years old, type: small 2 yrs
  • Male poodle, type: male poodle
  • Any dog less than 5 years old, type: 5 years
  • labradors that are more than 1 year old, type: labrador > 1 year
  • Search for a maltese poodle (maltipoo) maltese poodle

Why do your require a login to browse ?

To get the most out of the website we need to uniquely save each person's searches and favorites list, along with knowing how to notify them. We are assuming you are seriously considering adopting and the goal is to make it easier for you to find the dog of your choice.

What's with the facebook/google login ?

Rather than having to create yet another account password combination for you to forget, we use authentication services provided by facebook and google. These services use the latest secure OAuth-2 protocol. BTW, the first time you login facebook/google will inform you that kokowantsahome would like your email address. If you decline to do so, you will not be able to proceed. Your email is used for no other purpose other than for notification emails (not sold to telemarketers etc, if you haven't noticed there are no ads on this website)

What information can you access from my facebook/google account ?

Just your name and email address. We have no access to your posts, settings, friends etc.

What if I don't have either a facebook or google account ?

If you are still hiding under a rock, (and there are enough people like you) we may set up a way to register and create accounts on this site. Please contact us.

I no longer wish to receive notification emails, what do I do ?

In the browse page, click the accounts button and choose unsubscribe. Once you confirm the dialog, you will be removed from our user database. You can always come back, at that point you would be like a new user. We maintain no history of past users.

Why are the notification emails, from this weird "postmaster ..." ?

Did I mention I developed this website on my own without $$s. The email service used is mailgun. That's the free account, up to 10,000 emails per day in action.

Why doesn't this website load blazing fast ?

Running this with my own $$s, on a flexible cloud service. Don't want to waste $$s on unused capacity. That being said, please let me know if it is slow to the point of being unusuable. I can fork out my hard earned cash for additional webservers in the cloud (it's all very seamless, as long as you have a credit card !)

What is the function of the and icon on each dog's panel display ?

Clicking on the icon, adds the dog to your favorites list. Clicking the icon removes the dog from your list. To not display dogs that have selected, check the display filter option. A simple way to use this feature, is to browse the list of dogs and click on the icon for any that you like. Then at the shelter, you can view your favorites list and cross out the dogs that are not suitable by clicking the icon.

Which shelters are the animals listed from ?

Austin Animal Center.

Why aren't other rescue organizations included ?

While there are many pet rescue organizations doing great work, they often don't have a streamlined process to see the animals. The animals are usually fostered in different locations, which can result in a frustrating experience trying to adopt a pet. Some "rescue" organizations have fees that make you wonder if they are really breeders in disguise. That being said, if you feel strongly about any organization, please contact us.

Why have this website, when we can browse the center's website directly

You certainly can, but the Austin Animal Center website is seriously challenged, when it comes to executing intelligent searches based on breed, age etc. Also in an era of smartphones, their website is next to impossible to even browse for pets on your smartphone. The real power of koko-wants-a-home is the ability to save a search and then have an email notification sent to you when a dog matching your criteria arrives at the shelter. Koko-wants-a-home is also designed to be smartphone friendly.

What about other websites like petfinder.com ?

The biggest issue with these sites, is the information overload, since they pretty much accept adoption listings from any organization. Imagine searching for a particular breed and being shown a 1000 results! Based on our own experience, it was frustrating spending several days trying to get in touch with someone to see a dog we liked, only to be told the dog was unavailable. They aren't as bad to navigate on a smartphone as the Austin Animal Center's own website (but trust me, that's a low bar).

What is the adoption procedure ?

In general, you will be expected to meet with an adoption counselor and register. That way as soon as you find a dog that you are interested in, you call the shelter and they put you on the list of people wanting to adopt that dog. I think you have 24 hours to meet the dog, before they call the next person on the list. For updated information pleae check with the shelter.

What kind of dog should I adopt ?

There are a lot of websites with useful info, the adoption counselor can also guide you. The biggest consideration is probably size. If you have an apartment, a big dog can be challenging.

What is the financial commitment ?

I believe it is the law in Texas for all shelter animals to be spayed/neutered. So that should already have been done. The adoption fee at the Austin Animal shelter is $75 (check the shelter website for the up to date info). You have to budget for regular vetinerary care. Annual vaccines and checkup, shouldn't exceed $100. There are different types of pet insurance offered, but be aware that almost all have a deductible. A reasonably priced plan will not cover routine visits to the vet. (meant for accidents, surgeries etc)

What about traveling with my dog ?

Many hotels are pet friendly for an added fee. Some charge on a per night basis (+$10 to $30), others have a flat rate. check out bringfido for pet friendly accomodation. Within the US, small dogs can fly with you in the cabin for $125 each way (Southwest is slightly less). Your dog will need a doctors wellness certificate along with proof of having been vaccinated against rabies. International travel for vacations will generally be out of the question, since most countries have quarantine requirements. The one exception is travel to Canada, which has similar requirements to a domestic flight. Canada is also very pet friendly. Koko has vacationed in Banff and Victoria (Vancouver Island).

Which dog breeds are hard to come by ?

Small dogs like maltese, minature poodles, yorkies etc, hypoallergenic dogs (that don't shed hair) will often be adopted very quickly.

Why aren't there cats or other pets ?

This is the initial rollout, if there are enough people who think this would be useful for cats or other pets, we can add them too. Please contact us.
